About

Dr. Valeriia Tarasenko is a researcher and analyst specializing in public policy, leadership, and environmental sustainability. She holds a PhD in Public Policy from Fudan University, a Master’s Degree in Civil and Commercial Law from Shanghai University, and a Bachelor of Philology, Teacher of Chinese and English Languages & World Literature from the Kramatorsk Institute of Economics and Humanities.

Fluent in Ukrainian, Russian, English, and Chinese, with additional knowledge of German, she brings an interdisciplinary and international perspective to her work. Her mission is to bridge research, policy, and practice to advance sustainable governance, ecological responsibility, and global collaboration.

Patent

Device and Method for Organizing Laboratory Pipettes

Patent Number: CN ZL 2019 1 1198557.X

Granted: March 12, 2024

Invented by Jian Guo (郭剑) and Valeriia Tarasenko (丽娜)

Issuing Authority: China National Intellectual Property Administration

This invention emerged from practical research experience, where the use of pipettes is essential for conducting experiments, transferring liquids, and collecting data in fields such as environmental science, biology, and chemistry. Traditional storage methods often lead to disorganization, contamination, unnecessary resource use, and time inefficiency.

Our device addresses these challenges by improving laboratory workflows, minimizing cross-contamination risks, and significantly reducing material waste. By extending the life cycle of laboratory equipment and encouraging systematic use, the invention promotes ecological efficiency and supports the broader shift toward sustainable scientific infrastructure.

Moreover, the device plays a vital role in enhancing analytical methods. It increases the precision and efficiency of liquid transfer processes, which are critical for ecological data analysis, while reducing the time spent on tube organization by up to four times.

The broader environmental impact of the device lies in its ability to reduce laboratory waste and resource consumption. As research institutions worldwide strive to implement green policies and responsible innovation strategies, tools like this become essential for transforming laboratory culture and advancing ecological values within the STEM community.
